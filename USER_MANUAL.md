1.0 GENERAL INFORMATION

1.1 System Overview

BetterStreets is a mobile and web-based community reporting platform designed specifically for residents of Barangay Camaman-an, Cagayan de Oro City. The system enables residents to report street-level issues such as potholes, broken streetlights, illegal dumping, drainage problems, and other community concerns directly to barangay officials.

The platform consists of two main components:
- Mobile Application: Used by residents to submit and track reports
- Web Dashboard: Used by barangay administrators to manage and resolve reports

Key Features:
- GPS-enabled location tagging for accurate issue reporting
- Photo upload capability (up to 5 photos per report)
- Real-time status updates and notifications
- Offline mode with automatic synchronization
- Smart category suggestions based on report descriptions
- Interactive heatmap visualization of community issues
- Status history tracking for transparency
- Announcement system for community updates


1.2 Intended Users

The BetterStreets system is designed for two primary user groups:

Residents:
- Citizens of Barangay Camaman-an, Cagayan de Oro City
- Age 18 years and above
- Smartphone users (Android or iOS)
- Individuals who wish to report community issues and track their resolution

Administrators:
- Barangay officials and staff
- Authorized personnel responsible for managing community reports
- Users with access to desktop or laptop computers
- Individuals assigned to coordinate with barangay response units


1.3 System Requirements

Mobile Application Requirements:

For Android:
- Operating System: Android 5.0 (Lollipop) or higher
- RAM: Minimum 2GB
- Storage: At least 100MB free space
- Camera: Required for photo capture
- GPS: Required for location services
- Internet Connection: Required (WiFi or mobile data)
- Installation: APK installation or Expo Go app (for testing)

For iOS:
- Operating System: iOS 10.0 or higher
- RAM: Minimum 2GB
- Storage: At least 100MB free space
- Camera: Required for photo capture
- Location Services: Must be enabled
- Internet Connection: Required (WiFi or mobile data)
- Installation: TestFlight or Expo Go app (for testing)

Web Dashboard Requirements:
- Modern web browser (Chrome 90+, Firefox 88+, Safari 14+, Edge 90+)
- Screen Resolution: Minimum 1366x768
- Internet Connection: Required
- JavaScript: Must be enabled

To create a new resident account:

1. Launch the BetterStreets mobile application
2. On the welcome screen, tap "Sign Up"
3. Fill in the registration form with the following information:
   - Full Name: Enter your complete name
   - Email Address: Provide a valid email address
   - Phone Number: Enter your mobile number (format: 09XXXXXXXXX)
   - Address: Provide your complete address in Barangay Camaman-an
   - Password: Create a strong password (minimum 6 characters)
   - Confirm Password: Re-enter your password

4. Review the Terms and Conditions
5. Tap "Create Account" to submit your registration

Important Notes:
- Email address must be unique and not previously registered
- Password should contain a mix of letters and numbers for security
- Phone number must be a valid Philippine mobile number
- All fields marked with asterisk (*) are required


2.2 Email Verification

After registration, you must verify your email address:

1. Check your email inbox for a verification message from BetterStreets
2. Open the email and locate the 6-digit OTP (One-Time Password)
3. Return to the BetterStreets app
4. Enter the 6-digit OTP in the verification screen
5. Tap "Verify" to activate your account

Troubleshooting:
- If you don't receive the email within 5 minutes, check your spam folder
- Tap "Resend OTP" to request a new verification code
- OTP codes expire after 10 minutes
- Contact support if verification issues persist


2.3 Logging In

To log in to your BetterStreets account:

1. Open the BetterStreets mobile application
2. On the login screen, enter your registered email address
3. Enter your password
4. Tap "Login" to access your account

Session Management:
- Your session will remain active until you log out
- For security, you may be required to re-login after extended periods of inactivity
- The app remembers your login on the same device for convenience


2.4 Forgot Password

If you forget your password:

1. On the login screen, tap "Forgot Password?"
2. Enter your registered email address
3. Tap "Send Reset Link"
4. Check your email for password reset instructions
5. Follow the link provided in the email
6. You will be directed to the reset password screen


2.5 Resetting Your Password

To reset your password:

1. After clicking the reset link in your email, you will see the Reset Password screen
2. Enter the 6-digit OTP sent to your email
3. Enter your new password
4. Confirm your new password by entering it again
5. Tap "Reset Password" to save your new password
6. Return to the login screen and log in with your new password

Password Requirements:
- Minimum 6 characters
- Should not be the same as your old password
- Use a combination of letters and numbers for better security

5.1 Accessing the Report Form

To create a new report:

Method 1: From Home Screen
- Tap the large green "+" button in the center of the home screen
- Or tap "Create New Report" button

(Screenshot: Home screen with Create Report button highlighted)

Method 2: From Navigation Bar
- Tap the "Create" tab (plus icon) in the bottom navigation bar

Method 3: From Menu

5.2 Entering Report Details

The Create Report form includes the following fields:

(Screenshot: Complete Create Report form)

Title (Required):
- Enter a brief, descriptive title for the issue
- Examples: "Large pothole on Main Street", "Broken streetlight near park"
- Maximum 100 characters

(Screenshot: Title input field with example)

Description (Required):
- Provide detailed information about the issue
- Include specific location landmarks
- Describe the severity and impact
- Mention any safety concerns
- Minimum 20 characters, maximum 500 characters

(Screenshot: Description text area with example)

Category (Required):
- The system will suggest a category based on your description
- Available categories:
  * Road Damage (potholes, cracks, uneven surfaces)
  * Lighting Issues (broken streetlights, dark areas)
  * Waste Management (illegal dumping, uncollected garbage)
  * Drainage (clogged drains, flooding, water accumulation)
  * Sidewalk Issues (damaged sidewalks, obstructions)
  * Traffic & Safety (missing signs, hazards)
  * Vandalism (graffiti, property damage)
  * Other (issues not covered by other categories)

(Screenshot: Category dropdown menu)

Priority Level (Optional):
- Low: Minor inconvenience, not urgent
- Normal: Standard issue requiring attention
- High: Significant problem affecting safety or accessibility

(Screenshot: Priority selection dropdown)


5.3 Adding Photos

Photos provide visual evidence and help officials understand the issue better.

To add photos:

1. In the Create Report form, locate the "Add Photos" section
2. Tap "Add Photo" or the camera icon

(Screenshot: Add Photos section with camera icon)

3. Choose your preferred method:
   - Take Photo: Open camera and capture a new photo
   - Choose from Gallery: Select existing photos

(Screenshot: Photo selection dialog)

Photo Guidelines:
- You can add up to 5 photos per report
- Recommended photo size: Less than 5MB each
- Take clear, well-lit photos showing the issue
- Include multiple angles if necessary
- Capture the surrounding area for context

(Screenshot: Camera interface for taking photo)

Managing Photos:
- Tap the "X" icon on a photo to remove it
- Tap a photo thumbnail to view it full-screen
- The first photo will be the main display image

(Screenshot: Added photos with preview thumbnails)


5.4 Location Tagging

Accurate location information is crucial for issue resolution.

Automatic Location Detection:

1. When you open the Create Report form, the app will automatically attempt to detect your current location
2. You will see a loading indicator while GPS coordinates are being fetched

(Screenshot: Location loading indicator)

3. Once detected, your address will display in the Location field
4. The map will center on your current position with a pin marker

(Screenshot: Map with location pin and address displayed)

Manual Location Selection:

If automatic detection fails or you need to report an issue at a different location:

1. Tap the map to select a different location
2. Drag the pin marker to the exact position
3. The address will update based on the selected coordinates

(Screenshot: Map with draggable pin marker)

Location Requirements:
- Location must be within Barangay Camaman-an boundaries
- GPS/Location services must be enabled on your device
- Internet connection required for map loading


5.5 Category Selection

Automatic Suggestion:
1. As you type your description, the system analyzes keywords
2. A suggested category will appear automatically
3. Review the suggestion and accept or change it

(Screenshot: Category suggestion appearing below description)

Manual Selection:
- If the suggested category is incorrect, tap the category dropdown
- Scroll through the list of available categories
- Select the most appropriate category for your issue
- If unsure, select "Other" and provide detailed description

(Screenshot: Category dropdown with all options visible)

2. An offline indicator will appear at the top

3. Fill in all report information
4. Add photos from your device
5. Tap "Save as Draft" (Submit button changes when offline)

(Screenshot: Save as Draft button when offline)

Draft Management:
- Drafts are saved locally on your device

Issue: Cannot log in

- Internet connection issues

- Check internet connection

Possible Causes:
- GPS/Location services disabled
- Poor GPS signal
- App doesn't have location permission

Solutions:
- Enable Location Services in device settings
- Grant location permission to BetterStreets app
- Move to area with clear sky view
- Restart device GPS
- Try manual location selection on map


Issue: Photos won't upload
Possible Causes:
- Photos too large (>5MB each)
- Slow internet connection
- Storage permission not granted

Solutions:
- Compress photos before uploading
- Use device camera to take new photos (auto-compressed)
- Check internet connection speed
- Grant storage permission in device settings
- Try uploading fewer photos at once


Issue: Report stuck in draft
Possible Causes:
- No internet connection during submission
- Server temporarily unavailable
- Incomplete form data

Solutions:
- Check internet connection
- Open draft and verify all fields are filled
- Try submitting again
- Contact support if issue persists


Issue: Not receiving notifications
Possible Causes:
- Notifications disabled in app or device settings

- Push notification service unavailable

- Check notification settings in app (Profile → Settings)
- Enable notifications in device settings
- Disable battery optimization for BetterStreets
- Ensure app is updated to latest version
- Log out and log back in


Issue: App crashes or freezes
Possible Causes:
- Insufficient device memory
- Outdated app version
- Corrupted cache data

Solutions:
- Close other running apps
- Restart device
- Clear app cache (Settings → Apps → BetterStreets → Clear Cache)
- Update to latest version from app store
- Reinstall app if issue persists


Issue: Map not loading
Possible Causes:
- No internet connection
- Map service temporarily down
- Location services disabled

Solutions:
- Check internet connection
- Enable location services
- Try refreshing the map
- Restart app
- Check for app updates

11.0 FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)

Q1: Is BetterStreets free to use?
A: Yes, BetterStreets is completely free for all residents of Barangay Camaman-an.

Q2: Do I need to live in Barangay Camaman-an to use BetterStreets?
A: Yes, the system is designed specifically for residents of Barangay Camaman-an, Cagayan de Oro City.

Q3: Can I report issues anonymously?
A: No, you must create an account with verified email to submit reports. However, in the "All Reports" view, other users will not see your personal information.

Q4: How long does it take for my report to be addressed?
A: Response time varies depending on issue severity and complexity. Pending reports are typically reviewed within 1-2 business days. Emergency reports may be prioritized.

Q5: Can I edit or delete a report after submission?
A: Currently, reports cannot be edited or deleted after submission. If you need to make changes, contact barangay officials or submit a new report with corrected information.

Q6: What happens if I submit a duplicate report?
A: Duplicate reports will be identified by administrators and may be marked as duplicates or rejected. Check "All Reports" before submitting to see if the issue has been reported.

Q7: Can I report issues outside of Barangay Camaman-an?
A: No, BetterStreets is designed specifically for issues within Barangay Camaman-an boundaries. For issues outside the barangay, contact the appropriate local government office.

Q8: How do I know if my report has been received?
A: You will see a success message immediately after submission, and the report will appear in "My Reports" with status "Pending". You will also receive a confirmation notification.

Q9: Why do I need to verify my email?
A: Email verification ensures account security and allows us to send you important notifications about your reports.

Q10: Can I use BetterStreets on multiple devices?
A: Yes, you can log in to your account on multiple devices. Your reports and data will sync across all devices.

Q11: What if I don't have a smartphone?
A: BetterStreets is primarily a mobile application currently in testing phase. If you don't have a smartphone, you can visit the barangay office to report issues in person or call the barangay hotline.

Q12: Are my photos and personal information secure?
A: Yes, BetterStreets uses secure servers and follows data privacy best practices. Your personal information is not shared publicly, and photos are only used for issue verification.

Q13: Can I attach videos to my report?
A: Currently, only photos are supported. You can attach up to 5 photos per report.

Q14: What should I do if I see inappropriate content?
A: Use the "Report Issue" button on the report details page to flag inappropriate content. Administrators will review and take action.

Q15: How do I update my profile information?
A: Go to Profile tab → Edit Profile. You can update your name, phone number, address, and profile photo.

Q16: Can businesses or organizations use BetterStreets?
A: BetterStreets is designed for individual residents. However, businesses located in Barangay Camaman-an can create accounts to report issues affecting their premises.

Q17: What types of issues should I NOT report on BetterStreets?
A: Do not report:
- Emergencies requiring immediate response (call 911 or emergency services)
- Crime or suspicious activity (contact police)
- Personal disputes or complaints about individuals
- Issues outside Barangay Camaman-an
- Spam or advertising

Q18: How often is the heatmap updated?
A: The heatmap updates in real-time as new reports are submitted.

Q19: Can I volunteer to help resolve reported issues?
A: Contact the barangay office directly to inquire about volunteer opportunities. BetterStreets is a reporting platform and does not coordinate volunteers.

Q20: Will I be notified when my report is resolved?
A: Yes, you will receive a push notification and see the status change in your "My Reports" list when your report is marked as resolved.

12.0 GLOSSARY OF TERMS

Administrator:
A barangay official or staff member authorized to manage reports, update statuses, and post announcements through the web dashboard.

Announcement:
An official message posted by administrators to inform residents about community updates, events, or important information.

Barangay Unit:
A specific department or team within the barangay government responsible for addressing certain types of issues (e.g., Public Works, Sanitation).

Category:
A classification system for reports based on issue type (e.g., Road Damage, Lighting Issues, Waste Management).

Dashboard:
The main administrative interface (web-based) where administrators manage reports and view analytics.

Draft:
A report that has been created offline or saved but not yet submitted to the server.

GPS (Global Positioning System):
Technology used to determine the exact geographic location of a report for accurate issue mapping.

Heatmap:
A visual map representation showing the concentration and distribution of reported issues across the barangay.

In Progress:
A report status indicating that work has begun on addressing the issue.

Location Tagging:
The process of attaching GPS coordinates to a report to specify the exact location of the issue.

Notification:
An alert sent to users informing them of report status changes, new announcements, or other important updates.

OTP (One-Time Password):
A temporary 6-digit code sent via email for account verification purposes.

Pending:
A report status indicating that the issue has been submitted but not yet reviewed or assigned.

Priority:
The urgency level assigned to a report (Low, Normal, High) indicating how quickly it should be addressed.

Push Notification:
An instant alert that appears on a user's device even when the app is not actively open.

Report:
A submission detailing a community issue, including description, photos, location, and category.

Resident:
A citizen of Barangay Camaman-an who uses the BetterStreets mobile application to report issues.

Resolved:
A report status indicating that the issue has been successfully addressed and fixed.

Status:
The current state of a report (Pending, In Progress, Resolved, or Rejected).

Status Timeline:
A chronological history showing all status changes and updates made to a report.

Sync/Synchronization:
The process of uploading locally saved draft reports to the server when internet connection is restored.

User Profile:
A resident's account information including name, email, contact details, and report history.
